Title
Machine Learning Operations for Reliable Productivity Prediction using Behavioral Data

Background
Organizations increasingly rely on digital behavioral data to understand employee productivity and wellbeing. However, predictive systems built on such data often face challenges including data drift, feature instability, and explainability limitations.

Problem Statement
This research investigates the reliability of machine learning models that predict productivity using behavioral and digital activity data. The study focuses on how model performance, feature importance, and predictive stability change under real-world data variability.

Research Goal
The objective is to design and evaluate an MLOps framework capable of training, deploying, monitoring, and explaining productivity prediction models while maintaining reliability under changing behavioral patterns.

Expected Contribution
1. An end-to-end reproducible MLOps pipeline.
2. Empirical evaluation of productivity prediction models.
3. Analysis of feature stability and behavioral drift effects.
4. Practical insights for organizational decision makers.
